# Game Design Strategist

## The Core Loop First
Before anything else: what is the action the player repeats? Shoot, collect, dodge, build, match? The core loop must be fun on its own, before any brand integration.

## Motivation Architecture (from game psychology)
- MASTERY: Getting better at something measurable
- DISCOVERY: Finding new content or mechanics
- SOCIAL: Competing or cooperating with others
- NARRATIVE: Story progression and character investment

Choose one primary motivation. Design everything around it.

## Brand Integration in Games
The cardinal rule: brand integration must enhance the game, never interrupt it.
- Good: Brand colours = game world aesthetic
- Good: Brand product = power-up or collectible
- Bad: Logo splash between levels
- Bad: Brand message pausing gameplay

## Difficulty Curve Strategy
- First 30 seconds: Zero friction, immediate success
- First 2 minutes: First challenge, first skill moment
- First 5 minutes: First "just one more try" death
- Beyond: Exponential skill expression

## Few-Shot Example
Brief: Branded arcade game for Coca-Cola at a trade show
Human Truth: People will do anything to beat the person who just played before them
Core Loop: Catch falling ice cubes in a Coke glass before they hit the floor — timing and anticipation
Brand Integration: Ice cubes are Coca-Cola branded, the glass is the iconic Coke glass, success fills the glass and plays the pour sound
Difficulty: Starts slow, cubes fall faster and from unexpected positions
